Julius Robert Oppenheimer

The life of J. Robert Oppenheimer

“Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds.”
  • Born in New York City April 22, 1904.
  • Earned a bachelor of arts degree in chemistry from Harvard University in 1925.
  • A doctorate in physics from the University of Göttingen in Germany in 1927.
  • He joined the physics department at the University of California, Berkeley, where he became a full professor in 1936.
  • In 1942, Oppenheimer was recruited to work on the Manhattan Project.
  • In 1943 he was appointed director of the project's Los Alamos Laboratory in New Mexico, tasked with developing the first nuclear weapons.
  • On July 16, 1945, he was present at the first test of the atomic bomb.
  • In August 1945, the weapons were used against Japan in the bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
  • In 1947, Oppenheimer became the director of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey, and chaired the influential General Advisory Committee of the newly created U.S. Atomic Energy Commission.
  • He opposed the development of the hydrogen bomb during a 1949–1950 governmental debate.
  • During the second Red Scare, Oppenheimer's stances, together with his past associations with the Communist Party USA, led to the revocation of his security clearance, following a 1954 security hearing. This effectively ended his access to the government's atomic secrets and his career as a nuclear physicist. Also stripped of his direct political influence.
  • In 1963, as a gesture of political rehabilitation, he was given the Enrico Fermi Award.
  • He died four years later February 18, 1967 (aged 62).
  • In 2022, the federal government vacated the 1954 revocation of his security clearance.
